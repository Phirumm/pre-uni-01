drop cap

Drop caps are a traditional typographic technique where the first letter of a paragraph is enlarged and sometimes
decorated. They originated in medieval manuscripts where scribes would often illustrate the first letter of a chapter. In
modern design, drop caps provide visual interest and guide the reader's eye to the beginning of the text. They're particularly
effective for long-form content like articles, essays, and stories.
